Stellar Lumens (XLM) is a decentralized blockchain network designed to facilitate fast, low-cost cross-border transactions and enable the issuance of digital assets. Founded in 2014 by Jed McCaleb, co-founder of Ripple, the Stellar network employs its native cryptocurrency, Lumens (XLM), as a bridge asset to facilitate seamless exchanges between different currencies. It prioritizes scalability and inclusivity, aiming to provide financial services to unbanked and underbanked populations worldwide. Stellar's consensus protocol relies on the Federated Byzantine Agreement (FBA) algorithm, which enables quick transaction confirmation and increased network efficiency.

BLOCKCHAIN

Blockchain is a distributed and decentralized technology that enables secure and transparent peer-to-peer transactions and data storage. It consists of a chain of blocks, where each block contains a list of records (transactions) and a reference to the previous block, creating an immutable and tamper-resistant ledger. Blockchain's consensus mechanisms, such as Proof of Work (PoW) or Proof of Stake (PoS), ensure agreement among participants about the validity of transactions and the state of the ledger. This technology has gained popularity for its potential to revolutionize various industries, including finance, supply chain, healthcare, and more, by enhancing efficiency, trust, and accountability in digital interactions
